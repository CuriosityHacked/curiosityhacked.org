--- layout: blog published: true title: "St. Louis Guild 010 - First meeting: 5/14/13" ---

 

Last night we had our very first meeting and what a night!  The kids arrived and our Director Teri talked to them first.  She explained how the program was going to work, how they would learn at their own pace and what was expected of them.  She then introduced the mentors to the kids, we have four at the moment, Roger who is our Guild Leader, Jeff, Rachel and Nathan who all have skill sets that the scouts are going to need to learn.

Jeff then took them through a safety briefing which was absolutely brilliant, as well as learning important safety information about soldering and other things. They also learned what to do in the event that the laser sharks or laser cats get out of hand.  The kids are truly prepared.  They had a big surprise when they saw a special video message from Jeremy Blum (whose tutorial videos the kids are watching as part of the curriculum that Roger and the other mentors are creating) just for them. Roger then took them on a tour of the facility, and on to the uControl Hacker Space, where they got their Hacker kits. Wow, they were thrilled! You can watch it here: http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Iq09kciUbA8

The kids got straight to work writing their first Arduino sketch.  Apart from some computer issues that some had getting set up with the programming software, which kept our mentors running around helping, they all did great. Everyone completed the first lab with flying colors. They all got their lab signed off by a mentor and earned their first Knatom.  The older kids were so enthusiastic they went straight on and started Lab 2, which they will continue at home.

The mentors and helpers did a fantastic job and as a result this morning we have facebook posts all over saying how the kids are Hacker Scout Crazy and already back to work on the Lab 2 stuff at home. Long may the learning frenzy continue!

We are hoping to start our first wave of new scouts soon. We also have a Facebook group which will be kept updated and you are welcome to join.

If you would like to get involved as a mentor or as a parent with your child (age 8-18), please contact us and we will put you on the waiting list. info@stlhackerscouts.org
