Hacker Scouts Guild 001 is visited by the San Francisco Chronicle!

The word is getting out!  We will announce when the article is available for viewing.

 

 

 

 

 

 

Crews

The meeting started off with the crews getting together to make unique chants, and they worked on designing their logos on Inkscape.

 

 

 

 

   

 

Lesson in Power

Garratt taught the guild about AC power vs. DC.  He brought in a fried wall outlet and surge protector, and explained what voltages we use in outlets, power lines, household appliances and our hackerling shield.

 

 

~~~~SECOND WAVE~~~~

Second Wave worked on their Hackerling shields.

      

 

~~~~FIRST WAVE~~~~

Programming

Programmers continued working on their 'making change' program and next week we will start integrating with the Hackerling Shield.

   

2D to 3D

The 2D to 3D group is solidly into the prototyping phase and cutting/printing design prototypes now.  One scout cut the panels for her desk, and another scout 3D printed a 50% scale model of her bear.  The remaining scouts will be cutting their prototypes next week.

        

 

 

  LED

 The LED group explored different   applications with LED lights.  

 

 

 

They also worked with thermal plastic,

experimenting in ways to use it with LEDs.

 

 

 

The lightsaber project is nearly completed but at the last moment we caused a short in the LED controller circuit so we will fix that and be done next week.  Another scout began working on his Pi counter project, and he soldered the 7-segment display to the adafruit backpack.

        

 

CORE VALUE PERSISTENCE      

The final lesson on positive attitude was persistence.  The assignment for next week is to identify some activity that the scout has given up on, and give it another go.  Scouts should write down (or draw a picture about) the activity, why they stopped, and what they did differently this time.
