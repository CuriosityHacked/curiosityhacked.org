Hackerling Circuit Progress

[[{"fid":"304","view_mode":"full","type":"media","attributes":{"height":360,"width":480,"style":"width: 300px; height: 225px; float: right;","class":"media-element file-full"}}]]
We are all headed to the finish line on the Hackerling Circuit!  Many of the scouts started stitching their pouches, and some are almost done!  They riveted their loops on with brass rivets and pounded in their snaps!  The scouts are using a lock stitch for strength and durability.  A few are still working on their enclosures and will soon be done!  

 [[{"fid":"316","view_mode":"preview","type":"media","attributes":{"height":180,"width":180,"style":"width: 200px; height: 200px; float: left;","class":"media-element file-preview"}}]]

What an experience this has been for our Hackerscouts!  A project that involved soldering components, programming circuits, Computer aided design, laser cutting, construction of enclosure, leather working, textiles, stitching and riveting!  The scouts had to troubleshoot and use their resourcefulness, persistence, and creativity all along the way.

Creativity Activity

[[{"fid":"309","view_mode":"full","type":"media","attributes":{"height":360,"width":480,"style":"width: 250px; height: 206px; float: right;","class":"media-element file-full"}}]]

 

Samantha led the scouts in a Creativity activity where they had to ask their partner questions about their life and they had to draw images to represent what their partner described to them.  They were not allowed to use numbers or letters.  For example, when they asked how old they are, some wrote down dots to represent the years, some drew dominoes.  Some of the things they drew were pictures of family cars, siblings, and parents.  The scouts had to be descriptive, and the one drawing had to use their creativity to make visual representations of the information that was given to them.  This was challenging for them since they are used to being asked to describe things with words.  It was a great activity for the scouts to get to know each other better, and for them to use their thought processes in a different way.
