Second Wave Begin Hackerling Circuit

This was a pretty exciting meeting for the Second Wave Scouts!  They began soldering components on the Hackerling Circuit!  It was great to see them dive in and get to work, and they made a great start!  They are taking their time, learning the fundamentals of soldering, and what the components are all for.  They learned about the arduino micro processor, and what the accelerometer circuit is.  They are learning the concepts of circuitry while working on soldering the components one at a time to make sure they know what it is they are soldering, as well as making sure each component goes in the right place.

   

   

 

Zombies, Gummy Bears, Wolves, and Hamsters, OH MY!

The meeting started off with the different groups getting together to plan their group logo.  There were spikes, hamsters, TARDISes, wolves, and many more cool designs drawn tonight.  The energy in the room was palpable as their creative minds went to work.  All were drawing their ideas, then they collaborated together to come up with a final design.  The scouts will work further next meeting to simplify their designs so that patches can be made.  The groups are called The Iron Winged Hamsters, Zombie Gummy Bears of Doom, Tech Gods, Crew 4, and Doctor Wolves.

 

           

 

First Wave Scouts Make Progress in Badge Groups

In the LED group, one scout finished wiring his laser eye-beam headpiece.  It has two batteries, two LEDS and a switch.  All that's left is a little sewing to make it more wearable.  Another scout planned out a design for his "Pi counter", it will be an LED matrix that calculates and displays all the digits of pi.  He began collecting parts for that this week, and he will start the project next week.

   


The programming group continued reviewing data types with an understanding of memory consumption. Control structures were discussed such as If/Then statements and different ways to form loops. Next week we will continue with pseudo-code and a programming activity. 

 

 

 

 


 2D to 3D badge group discussed the projects the scouts wanted to make and drew preliminary sketches of their projects.  A few began using computer software to design their 3D project. 

 

    

Special Projects

Teen Guild Member and Mentor CamDAX began soldering his PCB boards for his Maker Faire Project "TARDIS Tesla".  He was successful in soldering SMD (surface mount) components, his first time ever to do this!
