What does Guild 024 do?

We have fun, make new friends and geek out!

We meet twice a month. Once a month we work on our Arduinos, with the aim of building a robot by the end of this academic year. For the other meeting, we often do a design/build challenge.

Last year, girls and boys in our group earned these badges: LED, Ohm’s Law, Catapult, Rocket and Oscilloscope.  (You can see these badges at adafruit.com)

Other fun activities we have planned for this year include a field trip to a Maker Lab and a hike at a local Forest Preserve.

In the picture, a Hacker Scout is demonstrating his moving sculpture.  We learned about cams and cam followers in a recent meeting and used them to create the moving sculptures.  This one as a terrific puffball guy with crazy hair and button eyes and it spins around as you turn the crank!

Who are the leaders?

I'm the administrative leader of Hacker Scout Guild 024 and brings with me 17 years of youth leadership through a large, national not for profit organization.  My experience as a metalsmith is very helpful when we do engineering challenges.

Terrific dad volunteers are heading up the Arduino portion of the program!
 
 
What else has this group done?
 
We're thrilled to have become an official part of the national Hacker Scout organization this fall.  Coming up in future posts will be a few pictures of some of what we did last year and some introductory information about Arduino, the microcontroller that we work with.
