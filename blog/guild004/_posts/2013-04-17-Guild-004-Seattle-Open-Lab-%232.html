Our second Open Lab at Metrix went much smoother than the first.  Having two sessions available, and using Eventbrite for reservations worked very well.  We were happy to see returning Hacker Scouts, welcome some new faces.

 

Two sessions allowed more kids and parents to learn or practice soldering in smaller groups.  

 

We did the birdhouse project again, as so many people weren’t able to make it last time.  Having a few pre-cut for those who wanted to get to work right away was helpful, and the other kids were fascinated watching their pieces being made on the laser cutter.  The kids were challenged figuring out how the pieces fit together like a puzzle.  They were a slightly tighter fit to put together this time.  We used an x-acto knife to trim them, which worked fine, but a small wood file would have been better, and safe enough for the kids to use themselves. Blue painter’s tape helped hold the pieces together nicely while the tacky glue dried.  This is a simple, yet fun project that will be available for future Open Labs.
