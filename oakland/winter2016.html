    In a continuation from last session with mentor Cara, this 10 week session will have students building a solid foundation in various aspects of game design and development while using RPG Maker to start designing and creating their own game. Ages 13 and up, younger with instructor permission. Kids do not need to have taken the previous class to take this session.

She graduated with a degree in Game Design Art and hopes to inspire others to get into games as well. She believes young people have some of the best game ideas but don’t always know how to execute them—that’s where this class comes in! Her favorite series is Legend of Zelda and she enjoys adventure, horror, and puzzle games.

    Chemistry

    A proton and a neutron are walking down the street.

    The proton says, "Wait, I dropped an electron help me look for it."

    The neutron says "Are you sure?" The proton replies "I'm positive."

    Would you like to understand why chemists find this joke hilarious but ultimately inaccurate? Join us for introduction to chemistry to find out! In our class we’ll dive deeply into the concepts of atomic structure, molecular bonding and polarity. We will engage in tests to compare the solubility of substances and discuss the ways matter is constantly interacting all around us. Selected readings will be provided as an introduction to the concept that will be covered in class. We will perform in class tests or experiments, with an optional writing portion for each experiment. This class will also integrate the CH Matter Badge, which includes objectives and projects that support a hands-on experience in understanding the fundamentals of Chemistry.
    Jade Rivera is an innovative educator, writer and coach. She proudly serves as a GHF Ambassador for neurodiverse families. Prior to her career in progressive education she was a chemist. In 2003 she earned a Fulbright scholarship for academic research in Thailand.
    Ages 11+ Thursdays 10-11:30am, $325 12 week session: 1/7/16- 3/24/16
    Register: Thur 10am

    Making Through History: Middle Ages

    Join us for an immersion of history, seen through the art, innovation, and inventions of the Middle Ages. Starting just after the fall of Rome and working our way to the Renaissance, we will research, explore, recreate, build, and appreciate medieval technology and aesthetics, while connecting design and ingenuity to modern day influence and function. Blacksmithing, warfare innovation, stained glass, engineering, herbalism, and more! We will use quality materials and traditional techniques to explore the ways cultures all over the world expressed and advanced themselves in the Medieval world.
    This class will be taught by mentor Samantha Cook, who is the founder of Curiosity Hacked and has been teaching for over 20 years. She has an extensive background in history, humanities, art, and making. (Making Through History: Renaissance will be offered Spring 2016)
    Ages 7 and up Friday 1pm-3pm, $395 1/22/16 through 3/25/16
    Register: Fri 1pm
