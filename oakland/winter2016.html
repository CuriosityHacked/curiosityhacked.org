Video Game Design and Development

Join mentor Cara for a 10 week exploration of character, environment, and gameplay design. Using game development programs with simple and visual scripting, kids will build a solid foundation in various aspects of game design and development while also building skills in their own areas of interest.
Cara Ricci started playing video games in second grade and has been in love with the medium ever since. She graduated with a degree in Game Design Art and hopes to inspire others to get into games as well. She believes young people have some of the best game ideas but don’t always know how to execute them—that’s where this class comes in! Her favorite series is Legend of Zelda and she enjoys adventure, horror, and puzzle games.

Intro to Minecraft Modding

Transform Minecraft into your canvas and paintbrush. You’ll learn the primary tools used by Minecraft creators and Youtubers to create games, art, simulations, gadgets and more inside the world of Minecraft.
You’ll be introduced to the basics of reprogramming Minecraft with modding using drag and drop programming, but that’s just the beginning. WorldEdit is an existing mod that is like 3D Photoshop for Minecraft, it lets you paint buildings into existence, then copy and paste them, or level whole areas. CommandBlocks and Redstone are like ingame mods, that use the logic from programming and electrical engineering to bring your visions to life.
Come learn to create your own games, art and more inside of Minecraft! Turn your favorite game into your favorite creative tool, and learn the basic skills necessary to use more complex creative tools.
This class is powered by a collaberation between Curiosity Hacked and TechLX.
Ages 8 and up; You must have your own computer based Minecraft account. BYO Laptop, or use one of ours. Two times offered; Mondays 1pm-3pm or Fridays 4pm-6pm, $275 Sept 14th - Nov 16th

Intro to Arduino Robotics

Learn the basics of electrical engineering, mechanical engineering, and programming. You’ll construct a wheeled robot, wire up its motors and sensors, then bring it to life with code. Though rather than focus in depth on the code, you will broadly learn the Arduino platform, working with existing code and modifying it to do what you want.
The Arduino platform is like Lego Mindstorms with the plastic shells taken off. Instead of being limited to Lego’s set of components, you can attach and wire up almost any electrical component you want. Though you’ll have a specific set of parts for use in the class and competition, you can bring your own components and if there’s time we’ll show you how to use them.
The course begins with the construction of your robot chassis. Then you attach and wire up the core components. Finally you work from sample code to get the robot to do what you want. And finally compete against your classmates in an autonomous robotics competition! Then take it home to continue hacking at home.
This class is powered by a collaberation between Curiosity Hacked and TechLX.

Making Through History: Ancients

Join us for an immersion of history, seen through the art, innovation, and inventions of the Ancient World. Starting with Prehistory and working our way to the fall of Rome, we will research, explore, recreate, build, and appreciate Ancient technology and aesthetics, while connecting Ancient design and ingenuity to modern day influence and function. We will use quality materials and traditional techniques to explore the ways cultures all over the world expressed and advanced themselves in ancient times.

Advanced Minecraft Modding

Take full control of Minecraft with a deep dive into Minecraft Modding. In this course we expect you are already familiar with existing mods like WorldEdit, and how to use CommandBlocks and Redstone. If you are ready to start creating your own mods, this is the course for you! You’ll start modding with drag and drop programming, but move onto writing JavaScript code. Maybe you’re thinking, “Hey! Minecraft’s written in Java not JavaScript.” You’re right! But there’s a wonderful mod called ScriptCraft that let’s you write server mods in JavaScript. It also let’s you build programmatically using invisible “drones” that do your bidding inside Minecraft.
Come learn game programming, by reprogramming your favorite sandbox game! You’ll host your own CraftBukkit/Spigot Server, running ScriptCraft and WorldEdit, then write your own mods for it.
This class is powered by a collaberation between Curiosity Hacked and TechLX.

Advanced Arduino Robotics

Learn to program robots while learning the basics of electrical and mechanical engineering! This course assumes you have some experience with programming or robotics already, and dives deep into writing code from scratch. At first you’ll work from sample code, then you’ll start creating entirely new sections of code yourself!
You’ll build your robot on the Arduino electronics platform, which is a perfect step between Lego Robotics and professional robotics. Instead of being limited to the Lego collection of electronics components, you can wire up and program almost any electrical component using your Arduino.
First you’ll construct the chassis of your robot, then attach and wire up the motors and sensors. Finally you’ll bring your robot to life with code, and prepare it to compete against your classmates’ robotic minions! Then take it home to continue hacking at home.
This class is powered by a collaberation between Curiosity Hacked and TechLX.
Ages 10 and up, materials fee included ($85) - Kids keep their robot! Wednesdays 6:30pm-8:30pm, $360 Sept 14th - Nov 16th
